How you can turn 12K to 168K, no funny business.

Sacrifice is hard but if you start sacrificing a little more now in your 20’s you’ll make a big difference for yourself in your retirement.

Ratings group Canstar out a report in 2014 that analysed 74 public super funds across Australia.

It showed a 25-year-old with $10,000 in super and earning $55,000 who put in an extra $200 per month into their fund over five years ($12,000 in total) will see their retirement savings jump $168,700 from $1,930,086 to $2,098,793.

Nice right?

On the other hand, if that same person waited until they were 50 to make those payments, they would need to contribute $79,000 or $1316 each month to reach the same balance.

Yes, sacrifice is hard but look where it can get you.

It’s essential to prioritise. In your 20’s there are other considerations such as obtaining career qualifications, saving for a house, affording a family, and your retirement.

$200 a month is the same as not buying lunch every day at work. That all you would need to do.

Canstar also took aim at super fees.

The report compared two 35-year-old’s earning $65,000 a year and with a starting super balance of $80,000. One person payed 0.7 per cent in administration fees and the other person payed 0.45 per cent.

The employee paying lower fees would end up with $1,211,051 at the retirement age of 65, while the other would be left with $1,146,746, a difference of $64,305.

In Australia there are 350 super funds including personal, corporate, public sector, and industry super funds.

Being in the wrong fund can mean tens of thousands of dollars less in your retirement.

Go and look into your super now. Start now and be thankful you did.
