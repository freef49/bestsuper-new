WILL ARNETT

Test your knowledge of super!

Question 1

What percentage is the minimum percentage of your earnings that an employer must contribute to your super?

  1. 10%
  2. 11%
  3. 9.5%
  4. 8.25%

Answer

An employ must contribute 9.5% of your wages into your super account

Question 2

How much per year does a couple need to have a comfortable retirement?

  1. About 50000 a year
  2. About 53000 a year
  3. About 78000 a year
  4. About 59000 a year

Answer

A couple in retirement require an after tax income of 58784 to live comfortably according to the ASFA Retirement Standard.

Question 3

What size lump sum do you need on retirement to live comfortably as a couple – assuming that you can get the aged pension?

  1. About $1.1 million
  2. About 640000
  3. About 400000
  4. About 720000

Answer

Around 640000 is necessary to provide a comfortable income assuming that you’re eligible for at least part of the aged pension. If you’re not eligible for the aged pension, you’ll require $1.1million to live comfortably

Question 4

Normally at what age can you first access your super?

  1. Your retired and older than 50
  2. Reached preservation age and you’ve retired
  3. Reached the Age pension age
  4. Older than 63

Answer

You need to have reached preservation age and retired. Depanding on when you were born the preservation age can be 55, 56, 57, 58, 59 or 60 years.

Question 5

How old do you need to be to claim the Age pension?

  1. 55 years for women, 65 years for men
  2. 65 for both men and women
  3. 85 years old
  4. 65 to 67 years, depending on when you were born

Answer

Depending on when you were born, your Age Pension age can begin at 65, 65.5 years, 66 years, 66.5 years or 67 years.

Question 6

If you’re a woman aged 65 on average, how much longer can you expect to live?

  1. About 22 years
  2. About 11 years
  3. About 37 years
  4. About 35 years

Answer

A woman aged 65 can expect to live until she is about 87 which is another 22 years according to the Australia Life Tables

Question 7

If you’re a man aged 65 on average, how much longer can you expect to live?

  1. About 22 years
  2. About 11 years
  3. About 19 years
  4. About 35 years

Answer

A man aged 65 can expect to live until he is about 84 which is another 19 years according to the Australia Life Tables

Question 8

What age must you retire from the work force?

  1. 65
  2. 60
  3. 70
  4. Never, you’re not required to retire

Answer

There is no requirement to retire at a certain age. You could work forever if you wanted to

Question 9

What is the cut off age after which, you’re unable to make voluntary contributions to your super?

  1. 50
  2. 70
  3. 75
  4. 80

Answer

At age 75 you can’t voluntarily contribute to your super however mandatory super contributions from working may be made

Question 10

After you die who can’t receive your super benefits as a tax-free lump sum?

  1. Spouse
  2. Children under 18
  3. Financially independent children over 18
  4. Financially dependent children over 18

Answer

If the recipient of your super is a financially independent child over 18 tax must be paid.
