Your super statement in terms you understand

At the end of every financial year, every working Australian receives a super statement in the mail.

Most people skim over it and file or throw it away, but not you after reading this article.

Contributions

Your super statement has the contributions make to it in black and white. Go over this. There have been cases of employer’s not putting in the correct amount - which should be 9.5% at the time of writing.

If your putting in more than the 9.5% then it’s important to make sure that those are going in as well.

Fees

Look at how much you’re being charged on things like investment charges and administrative fees. If you think that you’re paying too much it might be time to look around for a better super option.

The most important number to look at is the net return numbers. This is the amount that you’ve earned minus the fees and charges you’ve paid.

Account Balance

Your statement will show how well your fund has done as a percentage. It will also show you things like what type of investment profile that you have.

Look at your money, is it growing at an acceptable rate? What is an acceptable rate? Look at ASIC’s super calculator if you want some more information.

If your super needs to grow faster consider making extra contributions to achieve a retirement that you want.

Insurance Cover

What if you die or are permanently disabled?

Your super can provide you with various levels of cover. It’s important to review the amount that your covered for.

Ask yourself these questions:

  • Is there enough to cover to cover all of my debts?
  • Is this the level of over that I cover that I expected?

An email or phone call to your fund and save a lot of anguish later on.

Personal Details

Say it with me. Keep. Everything. Up. To. Date.

This is your livelihood when you finish work, and if you have insurance your stability.

Check your beneficiaries in the instance of your death, and whether or not you have provided your tax file number. If you haven’t provided your tax file number your likely to not be eligible for numerous tax concessions. You’ll be robbing yourself of extring money in your retirement.
