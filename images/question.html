Depending on you date of birth you can start drawing from your super from the ages of 60 – 55.

There are exceptions where your superannuation can be drawn from early, an example would be becoming disabled or in cases of severe financial hardship.

If you want to keep working after your “preservation age” you can go through the “transition to retirement” scheme. This allows you to draw some funds from your super but you can also keep working. It’s important to get financial advice if you are thinking of going down this route.

What happens to my super when I change jobs?

  1. You can leave the money in the super fund
  2. You can ask your new employer to pay contributions into your old fund (which they may or may not be able to do)
  3. You can transfer the money from your old fund to your new fund.

How much super do I need?

It depends.

It depends on how you want to live. If you want to live the high life you’re going to need a lot more than someone who owns a house that is self-sufficient.

A group called the Australian Prudential Regulation Authority estimates that people require an income or about 60%of their total annual salary in retirement. To achieve this they also estimate that this means that a person need approximates seven times their annual wage in at the beginning of retirement.

What if I change from full time to part time work?

Your employer must contribute at least 9.5% of your salary. The only exception is if your income is less than $450 per month. Only when you’re earning less than $450 a month will you stop receiving super payments.
